## Education / School Operations

### Student Incident Response

```markdown
# Student Incident Response

**Owner:** Dean of Students
**Applies to:** All faculty and staff

## Purpose
Ensure consistent, documented response to student behavioral incidents.

## When to Use
- Physical altercation between students
- Verbal threat or intimidation
- Property damage
- Any incident requiring parent notification

## Steps

### Step 1: Ensure immediate safety
Separate involved parties. If medical attention needed, call nurse (ext. 201) first.

**Expected result:** All students are safe and separated.

### Step 2: Document the incident within 15 minutes
Open [incident form system]. Fill in: date, time, location, students involved, witnesses, your account of what happened.

**If you witnessed the incident directly:** Write your account first, then interview students.
**If you did not witness it:** Interview witnesses first, then involved students separately.

**Expected result:** Incident form saved with basic facts.

### Step 3: Notify administration
Email [administrator] with incident form link. Include severity assessment: Low / Medium / High.

| Severity | Criteria | Response Time |
|----------|----------|---------------|
| Low | Verbal only, no prior history | End of day |
| Medium | Repeated behavior or minor physical | Within 2 hours |
| High | Injury, weapon, or threat of harm | Immediately |

### Step 4: Contact parents/guardians
Call (do not email) parents of all involved students within the response time above.

**Script:** "This is [name] from [school]. I'm calling because [student] was involved in an incident today. [Brief factual description]. [Student] is safe. Here's what happens next: [next steps]."

**Expected result:** Parents contacted and informed. Log call time in incident form.

### Step 5: Follow up within 48 hours
Schedule follow-up with involved students. Document any additional information or restorative actions.

## Verification
- [ ] Incident form complete with all fields
- [ ] Administration notified within required timeframe
- [ ] All parents/guardians contacted by phone
- [ ] Follow-up scheduled
```

### New Student Enrollment

```markdown
# New Student Enrollment

**Owner:** Registrar
**Applies to:** Front office staff

## Purpose
Process new student enrollments completely and consistently so no steps are missed.

## Prerequisites
- [ ] Parent/guardian has completed enrollment application
- [ ] Proof of residency provided
- [ ] Previous school records requested
- [ ] Immunization records on file or waiver submitted

## Steps

### Step 1: Create student record
Open [SIS system]. Select New Student. Enter demographics from enrollment application.

**Expected result:** Student ID generated.

### Step 2: Assign grade level and homeroom
Check transcript or age-appropriate placement. Confirm with [counselor] if placement is unclear.

**If student is transferring mid-year:** Check current class sizes before assignment.

### Step 3: Set up technology accounts
Submit IT request for: email, LMS access, device assignment.

**Expected result:** IT ticket created. Accounts active within 24 hours.

### Step 4: Schedule orientation
Contact family to schedule campus tour and meet-the-teacher.

### Step 5: Notify receiving teachers
Email homeroom teacher and all subject teachers with: student name, start date, any known accommodations.

## Common Mistakes
- **Forgetting to request previous school records** — Set a 5-day reminder if records haven't arrived.
- **Not checking immunization compliance** — Student cannot attend until records are complete or waiver is filed.
```

## Content / Publishing Operations

### Blog Post Publishing Checklist

```markdown
# Blog Post Publishing

**Owner:** Content Lead
**Applies to:** Anyone publishing to the website

## Purpose
Ensure every published post meets quality, SEO, and distribution standards.

## Prerequisites
- [ ] Draft reviewed by at least one other person
- [ ] All sources verified (links clicked, stats confirmed)
- [ ] Images/visuals prepared

## Steps

### Step 1: Final content review
Run the anti-slop filter. Check for banned phrases, structural tells, voice consistency.

**Expected result:** Draft passes voice and quality check.

### Step 2: Format for MDX
Add frontmatter (title, description, date, pillar, keywords, interlinks). Apply JSX components.

**Expected result:** Post renders correctly in local preview.

### Step 3: SEO/GEO optimization
- Primary keyword in title, first 100 words, and 1-2 H2s
- Meta description: 150-160 characters
- Core answer in first 50 words (for AI citability)
- FAQ section included (pillar posts only)

### Step 4: Publish
Merge to main branch. Verify live page loads correctly.

### Step 5: Distribute
- Generate LinkedIn post using practitioner-sharing style
- Create newsletter tease (if applicable)
- Schedule social posts for optimal times (Tue-Thu, 8-10 AM)

## Verification
- [ ] Live URL loads with correct formatting
- [ ] All links in post are working
- [ ] Social distribution scheduled or posted
- [ ] Analytics tracking confirmed
```

## General Operations

### Vendor Onboarding

```markdown
# Vendor Onboarding

**Owner:** Operations Manager
**Applies to:** Anyone engaging a new vendor or contractor

## Purpose
Ensure new vendors are properly vetted, documented, and set up for payment.

## Steps

### Step 1: Collect vendor information
Send vendor information form requesting: legal business name, tax ID, W-9, insurance certificates, contact information.

### Step 2: Verify compliance
- [ ] W-9 received and filed
- [ ] Insurance meets minimum requirements
- [ ] Background check completed (if working with students)
- [ ] Contract or agreement signed

### Step 3: Set up in financial system
Create vendor record in [accounting system]. Enter payment terms, bank information (if ACH).

### Step 4: Notify relevant staff
Email department head confirming vendor is approved and ready for purchase orders.

## Decision Points

| If... | Then... |
|-------|---------|
| Vendor works directly with students | Background check required before any access |
| Contract value > $10,000 | Board approval required |
| Vendor is sole-source | Document justification in vendor file |
```

## Template Customization Notes

- Replace bracketed text `[like this]` with your specific tools, names, and systems
- Add or remove steps based on your actual process
- Decision point tables should reflect YOUR escalation paths and thresholds
- Update role names to match your organization's titles

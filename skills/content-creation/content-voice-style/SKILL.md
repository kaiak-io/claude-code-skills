# Content Voice & Style

Quality guard for writing that sounds like a knowledgeable peer, not a corporate blog or AI output.

## Core Philosophy

1. **Start with something worth saying** — If you can't articulate what only YOU can say about this topic, you're not ready to write
2. **Say it in your voice** — Match the reader's internal monologue, not a press release
3. **Use AI to say it better** — Not to generate ideas, but to sharpen yours

## Anti-Slop Filter (Condensed)

These patterns instantly signal "AI wrote this." For the exhaustive list, see [anti-slop-full-list.md](references/anti-slop-full-list.md).

**Hard-banned phrases** (never use):
- "In today's fast-paced world/digital landscape..."
- "Game-changing," "revolutionary," "cutting-edge," "innovative"
- "Let's dive in," "Let's unpack this," "Let's explore"
- "Furthermore," "Moreover," "Additionally" as transitions
- "Here's the kicker" / "And the best part?"
- "It's not about X—it's about Y" (the false dichotomy closer)

**Structural tells** (never do):
- The Triple Header ("What is X? Why does X matter? How to do X?")
- The Hollow Transition ("Now that we've covered X, let's move on to Y")
- The Hedged Conclusion ("Ultimately, the best approach depends on your situation")
- Fragmented Pseudo-Profundity (Short. Sentences. That feel. Reflective. But say nothing.)

**What to do instead:**

| AI Pattern | Human Alternative |
|------------|-------------------|
| "There are several reasons why..." | Jump straight to reason #1 |
| "It's worth noting that..." | Just state the thing |
| "Many experts believe..." | Name the expert or cut it |
| "In conclusion..." | No announcement needed — just conclude |
| "Research shows that..." | Name the specific research |

## Bold-First Rule

Every bullet point starts with the insight in **bold**. Readers scanning should get value from bold text alone.

**Bad:** You should always test your headlines before publishing to see which ones perform better.

**Good:** **Test headlines before publishing.** A/B test 2-3 options; pick the winner after 100 impressions.

## Personal Narrative Integration

Your best posts work because you're present in them. Generic advice doesn't land; your experience does.

### Hook Pattern

Start with a specific moment, not a thesis statement:

```
Good: Last month, a Head of School asked me a question that stopped me cold.
Good: I used to delegate technology decisions entirely to my IT coordinator.

Bad:  AI is transforming education in unprecedented ways.
Bad:  In the rapidly evolving landscape of educational technology...
```

### Personal Moment Types

Every post should have 2-6 personal narrative moments (scale with length):

- **The confession** — "A confession: I was skeptical that AI could handle nuanced parent communication."
- **The mistake** — "I used to delegate technology decisions entirely to IT. This worked for network infrastructure. It doesn't work for AI."
- **The realization** — "Here's what I've learned since: AI isn't a technology skill. It's a leadership skill."
- **The specific example** — "Then I tested it on a tricky email about a student behavior issue. The draft was 80% there."
- **The shift** — "When I realized AI literacy wasn't optional, I didn't sign up for a course. I started using the tools."

## Voice Preservation

### Tone Markers

- **Direct but warm** — Not cold/corporate, not overly casual
- **Peer-to-peer** — You're the knowledgeable colleague, not the guru
- **Specific over general** — Numbers, names, concrete examples
- **Permission to be opinionated** — Take a stance, don't hedge everything

### Phrases That Sound Human

- "Here's the thing..."
- "I've seen this go wrong when..."
- "The real problem is..."
- "What actually works is..."
- "Let me be specific..."
- "This sounds obvious, but..."
- "I understood the question. I'd asked it myself."

### Calibration Questions

Ask periodically while drafting:
- "Does this sound like something I'd actually say?"
- "Would I be embarrassed to read this aloud?"
- "Is this how I'd explain it to a peer over coffee?"
- "Where's my experience in this section?"

## Pattern Staleness

Even good moves become tics if overused. Rotate these — don't use the same one in consecutive posts:

- "This isn't X. It's Y." — the reframe
- "The [adjective] truth:" — the reveal
- Numbered shifts/principles (Shift 1, Shift 2...)
- Colon-after-bold headers (**The vision:** / **The reality:**)
- "Three questions that..." — the framework tease

**Self-check:** Scan your last 3 posts. If the same structural move appears in all of them, vary this one.

**The intro test:** Read your last 3 opening paragraphs back-to-back. Do they sound like the same template with different words? If yes, rewrite.

## Section Feedback Framework

When reviewing a section, evaluate:

```markdown
## Feedback: [Section Name]

### What Works
- [Specific strength]

### Sharpen These
- **Vague claim** → Make specific: [suggestion]
- **Missing evidence** → Add: [what's needed]
- **AI-sounding phrase** → Replace with: [alternative]
- **Buried lead** → Move this up: [the real point]

### Voice Check
- Sounds like you: Yes/No
- Tone match: [assessment]
- Personal narrative present: Yes/No
```

## Hook Improvement

When a hook is weak, offer 3 alternatives:

**Option 1 — The Specific Problem:** Hook that names exact pain point
**Option 2 — The Surprising Data:** Hook with counterintuitive stat or fact
**Option 3 — The Micro-Story:** Hook with brief narrative setup

All three should start with a specific moment, not a thesis statement.

## Final Quality Checklist

### Content Quality
- [ ] Every claim is specific (no "many," "various," "several")
- [ ] Examples are concrete and named
- [ ] The "so what" is clear within first 3 paragraphs
- [ ] Takeaways are actionable

### Anti-Slop
- [ ] No banned phrases (run against full list)
- [ ] Transitions are invisible (not announced)
- [ ] Varied sentence structure
- [ ] No hedging in conclusion

### Voice
- [ ] Sounds like peer-to-peer conversation
- [ ] Would read naturally aloud
- [ ] Personality comes through

### Personal Narrative
- [ ] Hook starts with specific moment (not thesis)
- [ ] 2-6 personal narrative moments included (scaled to length)
- [ ] At least one confession or mistake
- [ ] Voice markers present

### Pattern Staleness
- [ ] Scanned last 3 posts for repeated structural moves
- [ ] This intro doesn't mirror the last one
- [ ] Varied callout titles
- [ ] Different section rhythm than recent posts
